### **Knowledge Base: Machine Learning Fundamentals**

**1. The Fundamental Shift (Programming vs. Learning)**
*   **Traditional Programming:** Operates on explicit, human-written rules. A programmer provides the logic: *"If X happens, do Y."*
*   **Machine Learning (ML):** Operates on discovery. Instead of providing rules, we provide **Data** (examples) and an **Algorithm** (a flexible mathematical formula). The computer uses the algorithm to discover the rules/patterns hidden within the data itself.

**2. The Three Pillars of ML**
*   **Data:** The "textbook" for the computer. It consists of thousands of examples that the system studies to understand a specific task.
*   **The Algorithm:** The "learning engine." It is a mathematical framework that adjusts itself as it processes more data.
*   **Training:** The iterative process of learning. The computer looks at the data repeatedly, making guesses and refining the algorithm until it becomes accurate at recognizing patterns.

**3. The Primary Goal: Prediction**
*   The ultimate purpose of ML is **Generalization**. This means the system should be able to take **new, unseen data** and make a correct prediction or decision based on the patterns it learned during the training phase.
